About Me

Passionate Educator. Inclusive Advocate. Lifelong Learner.

Chifuniro Banda

Chifuniro Banda is a passionate and impact-driven education professional from Malawi with a Bachelor of Arts in Education (Literature and Linguistics) from Malawi Assemblies of God University (MAGU). He has experience in secondary school teaching, educational administration, literacy development, and advocacy for learners with dyslexia and special educational needs through Able Foundation - Dyslexia Malawi.

His professional journey is defined by a deep commitment to creating learning environments where every child — regardless of cognitive, linguistic, or physical difference — can thrive. He believes that education is the most powerful lever for social transformation in underserved communities.

Mission: To champion inclusive, equitable, and accessible education for every learner in Malawi and across Africa.

Professional Philosophy

What Drives My Work

Core beliefs that shape my approach to education, advocacy, and leadership.

Every child deserves not just access to education, but access to an education that sees them, values them, and empowers them. Inclusion is not a program — it is a promise.

Inclusive Education

I believe classrooms must be designed for all learners — including those with dyslexia, learning disabilities, and diverse cognitive profiles. Inclusion is not about fitting learners into the system; it's about reshaping the system to fit every learner.

Equitable Access

Equity means providing every child with what they need to succeed — not giving everyone the same thing. I advocate for differentiated resources, tailored instruction, and policy that prioritises the most marginalised.

Learner-Centred Teaching

Effective education places the learner at the centre. I design lessons that honour students' lived experiences, cultural backgrounds, and individual learning styles — fostering agency, curiosity, and growth.

Literacy Empowerment

Literacy is the gateway to all learning. My work focuses on evidence-based literacy intervention, especially for struggling readers, using structured and multi-sensory approaches that build confidence and competence.

Special Needs Advocacy

I am committed to raising awareness about dyslexia and other learning differences in Malawi — a context where such conditions are often misunderstood, stigmatised, or simply ignored.
